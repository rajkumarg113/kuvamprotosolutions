Die Moulding Manufacturing

Challenges

  • Long development cycles from design to tool readiness
  • High rework and rejection due to dimensional inaccuracies
  • Difficulty in optimizing mould life and repeatability
  • Lack of integration between design, prototyping, and production
  • Rising tooling costs impacting overall product profitability

Solutions

  • Custom Die & Mould Design optimized for manufacturability and tool life
  • Prototype to Production Tooling support for low- to high-volume manufacturing
  • High-Precision CNC Machining & EDM for complex geometries
  • Material Selection & Heat Treatment Guidance for durability and performance
  • Validation, Trials & Iteration Support to ensure first-time-right tooling

Blow Mould Die

Progressive dies perform a series of operations in a single die at two or more stations during each press stroke to form or make parts from a strip or coil of metal.

The strip is fed through the die with each press stroke, and with each stroke, a different operation is performed until the finished part is made.

Family Mould Die

Compound dies perform two or more cutting operations at a single station with a single stroke of the press. These dies are used when high accuracy between features is required.

The main advantage of compound dies is that they produce parts with very close tolerances between cut features since all cutting is done in one operation.
